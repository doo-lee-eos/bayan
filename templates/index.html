MORE THAN A DICTIONARY

Learn Arabic by making connections

Look up one word and you don't just get a definition — you get its whole family. Every occurrence traces back to the same three-letter root, so patterns start to jump out on their own: مُؤْمِن "believer" and إِيمَان "faith" share the same root as أَمَانَة "trust." Once you've noticed one connection like that, you start noticing them everywhere in the text.
